## School Selection Criteria

### Geographic Diversity
- ✅ **Urban:** 2 schools (Lagos, Abuja)
- ✅ **Sub-urban:** 1 school (Ibadan, Port Harcourt)
- ✅ **Rural:** 1 school (Smaller town/village)

### School Type Diversity
- ✅ **Public:** 2 schools
- ✅ **Private:** 2 schools
- ✅ **Mission/Faith-based:** 1 school (optional)

### Infrastructure Requirements
- ✅ **Basic:** Computer lab OR students with personal devices
- ✅ **Internet:** At least intermittent connectivity (test offline mode)
- ✅ **Electricity:** Regular OR generator/solar backup
- ✅ **Minimum:** 1 computer per 5 students OR BYOD (tablets/phones)

### Student Population
- ✅ **Class Size:** 20-40 students per class
- ✅ **Target Classes:** SS1, SS2, SS3
- ✅ **Minimum:** 60 students total (20 per class)
- ✅ **Maximum:** 200 students total for manageability

### Teacher Engagement
- ✅ **Requirement:** At least 3 committed teachers
- ✅ **Subjects:** Mathematics, English, Science (Physics/Chemistry/Biology)
- ✅ **Time:** Willing to dedicate 2-3 hours/week for monitoring
- ✅ **Training:** Available for 1-day training workshop

### Principal/Admin Support
- ✅ **Buy-in:** Principal enthusiastic about pilot
- ✅ **Flexibility:** Willing to adjust schedule for testing
- ✅ **Communication:** Reliable point of contact

## Recruitment Process

### Phase 1: Initial Contact (Week 1)
1. **Identify Schools:** Review list, get recommendations from educators
2. **First Contact:** Email principals with pilot program overview
3. **Follow-up:** Phone calls to interested schools
4. **Shortlist:** Select 5-7 interested schools

### Phase 2: School Visits (Week 2)
1. **Site Assessment:** Visit each school, check infrastructure
2. **Meet Stakeholders:** Principal, teachers, IT staff
3. **Presentation:** Demo system, explain pilot program
4. **Q&A Session:** Address concerns, clarify expectations

### Phase 3: Selection & MoU (Week 3)
1. **Select Schools:** Choose final 3-5 based on criteria
2. **Memorandum of Understanding:** Sign agreement outlining:
   - Pilot duration and scope
   - Responsibilities (school vs ExamsTutor)
   - Data privacy and NDPR compliance
   - Success metrics
   - Support provided
3. **Technical Setup:** Install necessary software, configure network

### Phase 4: Training & Launch (Week 4)
1. **Teacher Training:** 1-day workshop per school
2. **Student Orientation:** Introduction session
3. **Soft Launch:** Week 1 with close monitoring
4. **Full Launch:** Week 2 onwards

## Pilot Program Structure

### Duration: 4 Weeks

#### Week 1: Onboarding & Familiarization
- **Goals:** Get everyone comfortable with the system
- **Activities:**
  - Teacher training (Day 1)
  - Student accounts setup (Day 2)
  - Guided practice sessions (Days 3-5)
  - First diagnostic tests
- **Support:** Daily on-site support from ExamsTutor team

#### Week 2: Active Usage
- **Goals:** Regular, independent usage
- **Activities:**
  - Students use for homework/practice
  - Teachers assign questions
  - Begin analytics review
- **Support:** Remote support + 2x/week check-ins

#### Week 3: Deep Engagement
- **Goals:** Full feature utilization
- **Activities:**
  - Learning plan completion
  - Adaptive practice sessions
  - Teacher dashboard utilization
  - Parent access (if available)
- **Support:** Weekly check-ins + on-demand support

#### Week 4: Assessment & Feedback
- **Goals:** Measure impact, collect feedback
- **Activities:**
  - Post-pilot assessments
  - Teacher interviews
  - Student surveys
  - Data analysis
  - Case study documentation
- **Support:** Full-day visit for final review

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Poor Internet Connectivity
**Mitigation:**
- Prioritize offline mode
- Pre-load content on devices
- Provide mobile data stipends
- Schedule sync during stable hours

### Risk 2: Low Digital Literacy
**Mitigation:**
- Extended training sessions
- Peer mentoring (tech-savvy students help others)
- Simplified UI mode
- Visual guides and videos

### Risk 3: Low Teacher Adoption
**Mitigation:**
- Comprehensive training
- Show clear time-saving benefits
- Provide incentives
- Collect and act on feedback quickly

### Risk 4: Technical Issues
**Mitigation:**
- Thorough alpha testing first
- Dedicated support team
- Backup plans for critical features
- Regular monitoring

### Risk 5: Student Disengagement
**Mitigation:**
- Gamification elements
- Regular progress feedback
- Teacher encouragement
- Peer competition (optional)
