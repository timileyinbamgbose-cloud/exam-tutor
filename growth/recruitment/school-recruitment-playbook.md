### Stage 2: Lead Qualification (Days 1-3)

**Qualification Criteria (BANT Framework):**

**Budget:**
- Private schools: Can afford ‚Ç¶500/student/month
- Public schools: Seeking free/sponsored options
- Budget holder: Principal/Proprietor/Bursar

**Authority:**
- Decision maker identified (Principal/Proprietor)
- Timeline for decision (urgent vs. exploratory)

**Need:**
- Student performance challenges
- Exam preparation focus (WAEC/JAMB)
- Tech-forward mindset
- Current pain points (teacher bandwidth, student engagement)

**Timeline:**
- Ready to start: This term/semester
- Planning stage: Next term
- Just exploring: Future consideration

**Qualification Questions:**
```
1. How many students do you have in SS1-SS3?
2. What are your biggest challenges in preparing students for WAEC/JAMB?
3. Do students have access to devices (phones/tablets/computers)?
4. Is internet connectivity available (school/home)?
5. Who makes decisions about ed-tech purchases?
6. What's your budget for educational tools per student?
7. When would you like to start if we're a good fit?
```

**Disqualification Criteria:**
- <20 students in SS1-SS3 (too small)
- No device access and no plan to provide
- No internet and unwilling to try offline mode
- No budget and not eligible for sponsorship
- Decision timeline >6 months

### Stage 3: Initial Pitch (Days 4-7)

**Pitch Formats:**
1. **Phone call** (15 min) - Quick intro
2. **School visit** (45 min) - Full presentation
3. **Webinar** (30 min) - Group pitch for multiple schools

**Pitch Structure (45-minute school visit):**

**1. Opening (5 min)**
- Introduce yourself and ExamsTutor AI
- Ask permission: "Is now still a good time?"
- Set agenda: "I'll show you how ExamsTutor is helping students like yours improve scores by 18%"

**2. Problem Discovery (10 min)**
- "Tell me about your students' biggest challenges with WAEC/JAMB prep"
- "How many of your students pass WAEC on first attempt?"
- "What tools are you currently using for exam preparation?"
- "What frustrates you about those tools?"

Listen actively. Take notes. Identify pain points.

**3. Solution Presentation (20 min)**
- **Live Demo** (most important!)
  - Show student asking a question ‚Üí AI explanation
  - Show diagnostic test ‚Üí learning plan generation
  - Show teacher dashboard ‚Üí student insights
  - Show progress tracking ‚Üí improvement visualization

- **Pilot Results** (social proof):
  - "5 schools, 445 students"
  - "78% weekly active users"
  - "18% average score improvement in 4 weeks"
  - "4.3/5 teacher satisfaction"
  - "One student went from 38% to 62% in math in just 4 weeks"

- **Key Features**:
  - 24/7 AI tutor (unlimited questions)
  - Diagnostic tests (identify weaknesses)
  - Adaptive practice (personalized difficulty)
  - Learning plans (structured study path)
  - Teacher dashboard (real-time insights)
  - Offline mode (works without internet)
  - WAEC/JAMB aligned

**4. Addressing Concerns (5 min)**
Common objections and responses (see below)

**5. Next Steps (5 min)**
- "Based on what you've seen, is this something that could help your students?"
- If yes: "Great! Let's schedule a follow-up to discuss pricing and get started."
- If maybe: "What questions do you have? What would make this a definite yes?"
- If no: "I understand. Can you tell me what's holding you back?"

**Call to Action:**
- Schedule follow-up meeting
- Provide case study / pilot results document
- Offer free 1-month trial

### Stage 5: Objection Handling

**Objection 1: "This is expensive"**

**Response:**
"I understand budget is a concern. Let's look at the ROI:
- Your students' performance improves ‚Üí better WAEC/JAMB results ‚Üí higher school reputation ‚Üí more enrollments
- Teachers save 3+ hours/week ‚Üí can teach more effectively ‚Üí happier teachers
- At ‚Ç¶500/month per student, that's ‚Ç¶17/day. Less than a sachet of pure water. But the impact on their future is priceless.
- Plus, we offer a free first month to prove value. If it doesn't work, cancel anytime. No risk.
- And annual payment saves you 20%."

**Alternative offers:**
- Start with one class (SS3) to prove value, then expand
- Phased rollout: Start small, scale as budget allows
- Explore corporate sponsorship or NGO funding
- For public schools: Apply for government subsidy or go with free tier

---

**Objection 2: "We don't have devices/internet"**

**Response:**
"Great question! We designed ExamsTutor specifically for Nigerian schools, knowing infrastructure varies:

**Devices:**
- Students can use personal phones (most SS2-SS3 students have phones)
- School computer lab (even 10-15 PCs is enough for rotating access)
- We have partnerships with Tecno/Samsung for subsidized education devices

**Internet:**
- Offline mode: Download content in computer lab, use at home without internet
- Our pilot school in Owo had similar challenges - offline mode was a game-changer
- We have MTN/Airtel education data bundles (‚Ç¶200 for 1GB - 50% discount)
- Zero-rated domain (no data charges for *.examstutor.ng)

Let's schedule a site visit to assess your infrastructure and customize a solution."

---

**Objection 3: "Teachers might resist technology"**

**Response:**
"That's actually a common concern that we've solved! Here's how:

- We provide 2-day comprehensive teacher training (not just a manual)
- Teachers love the dashboard because it shows them exactly who needs help and with what
- ExamsTutor doesn't replace teachers - it amplifies them. Teachers in our pilot saved 3 hours/week on repetitive explanations
- 95% of our pilot teachers use the dashboard regularly, and 90% want to continue
- We provide ongoing support (WhatsApp group, weekly office hours, on-demand help)

In fact, teachers become your biggest advocates because they see the impact on their students."

---

**Objection 4: "We already use [Competitor]"**

**Response:**
"That's great that you're already using EdTech! What do you like about [Competitor]? What's missing?"

[Listen to their response]

"Here's what makes ExamsTutor different:
- **Offline mode** (most competitors require constant internet)
- **Nigerian curriculum focus** (specifically built for WAEC/JAMB, not adapted from foreign content)
- **Teacher dashboard with actionable insights** (not just student-facing)
- **Proven results**: 18% improvement in 4 weeks from our pilot
- **Local support**: We're based in Nigeria, understand your context

Would you be open to a side-by-side comparison? We can run a pilot with one class for free."

---

**Objection 5: "We need to think about it"**

**Response:**
"Absolutely, this is an important decision. To help you think through it, can I ask:
- What specific aspects do you need to think about? (uncover real objection)
- What information would help you make a decision?
- Is there anyone else who needs to be part of this conversation?
- What's your timeline for making a decision?

[Address specific concerns]

How about this: Let's start with a free trial - zero risk, no credit card. You can evaluate with your teachers and students, see the results firsthand, then decide. Fair?"

### Stage 6: Closing the Deal (Days 11-14)

**Closing Techniques:**

**1. Trial Close**
"If we can address [their main concern], would you be ready to move forward?"

**2. Assumptive Close**
"Sounds like ExamsTutor is a great fit for [School Name]. Let's get the paperwork started. When would you like to begin training - next week or the week after?"

**3. Urgency Close**
"We're onboarding schools now for the new term. If we sign by Friday, we can have you fully set up before term starts. After that, the next training cohort is in 3 weeks."

**4. Pilot Close**
"Let's start with SS3 this term since they're facing WAEC soon. If they improve as much as our pilot students (18% on average), we can expand to SS1 and SS2 next term. Sound good?"

**Required Documents:**

**1. Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)**
- Contract terms
- Pricing and payment schedule
- Responsibilities (school vs ExamsTutor)
- Data privacy and NDPR compliance
- Support SLA
- Cancellation policy

**2. Account Setup Form**
- School details (name, address, contact)
- Student count per class (SS1, SS2, SS3)
- Subject preferences
- Teacher details (names, emails, subjects)
- Billing information

**3. Data Privacy Consent**
- Parent/guardian consent for student data collection
- NDPR compliance acknowledgment
- Data usage policy

**Signatures Required:**
- Principal/Proprietor
- ExamsTutor representative

**Payment Processing:**
- Invoice sent immediately after MoU signing
- Payment due before training (prepaid model)
- Subsequent months: Auto-billing on 1st of month

## Regional Considerations

### Lagos/Ogun
- **Market:** Highly competitive, many schools, high expectations
- **Strategy:** Emphasize premium features, advanced analytics, parent dashboard
- **Pricing:** Full price (‚Ç¶500/student/month)
- **Challenges:** High acquisition cost, competitive pressure
- **Opportunity:** Large market, willing to pay, strong referral potential

### Abuja/FCT
- **Market:** Government focus, mix of public and private
- **Strategy:** Government partnerships, dual pitch (public/private)
- **Pricing:** Mixed (public free tier, private premium)
- **Challenges:** Bureaucracy for public schools, long sales cycle
- **Opportunity:** Government contracts, scalable

### Rivers/Delta
- **Market:** Oil-rich region, good private schools, internet challenges
- **Strategy:** Emphasize offline mode, proven results in similar contexts
- **Pricing:** Full price, flexible payment terms
- **Challenges:** Infrastructure (internet, power), distance
- **Opportunity:** Underserved market, high need

### Kano/Kaduna
- **Market:** Large student population, Islamic schools, lower income
- **Strategy:** Affordability, NGO sponsorships, cultural sensitivity
- **Pricing:** Flexible, sponsorships, free tier promotion
- **Challenges:** Cultural fit, language (Hausa support?), lower budgets
- **Opportunity:** Massive scale potential, untapped market

### Oyo/Osun
- **Market:** University towns, education-focused, mix of urban/rural
- **Strategy:** Academic credibility, referrals from pilot (Loyola Ibadan)
- **Pricing:** Standard, discounts for multiple schools from same proprietor
- **Challenges:** Mid-tier budgets, high expectations
- **Opportunity:** Education-conscious population, good infrastructure

## Best Practices

### Do's ‚úÖ
- **Personalize every pitch** - Reference their specific challenges
- **Demo live, not slides** - Seeing is believing
- **Lead with results** - "18% improvement" is powerful
- **Use pilot testimonials** - Social proof builds trust
- **Follow up promptly** - Strike while iron is hot (24-48 hours max)
- **Make it easy to say yes** - Free trial removes risk
- **Build relationships** - Education is relationship-driven
- **Listen more than talk** - Understand their needs first

### Don'ts ‚ùå
- **Don't oversell** - Be honest about limitations
- **Don't badmouth competitors** - Stay professional
- **Don't rush the close** - Respect their decision timeline
- **Don't ignore objections** - Address concerns head-on
- **Don't promise what you can't deliver** - Underpromise, overdeliver
- **Don't ghost after sale** - Onboarding is critical
